The Ratty's

Food Disposal Station:

IT'S TERRIBLE!

Overview

The Ratty's food disposal area is made of two parts. Part one is the composting section and part two is the place where cutlery and crockery is placed. These modules are stacked on both sides of an aisle seperated by a walkway.

2. Cutlery & Crockery

This area is where people sort their cutlery & crockery into the correct corresponding bins.

1. Composting

This area is where people scrape their remaing food waste into the bin. People also through away non-resusable cutlery and crockery.

Visual Observations

  • Users with non-resuable plates skipped station two entirely.
  • At frequenty class times (1 pm), long lines form.
  • Sometimes compost cans from station one are covered with plastic and are unusable.
  • On occasion dining center staff stand at the end of the station and help students sort plates.

Hands On Interviews

On average, how many times do you visit the Ratty per week?

+
The interviewed users tended to go to the Ratty 8-12 times a week.

On a scale of 1-6, how frequently do you get food in the Ratty and eat elsewhere?

+
Only one of the users interviewed takes food out of the Ratty. They answered a frequency of 2.

Do you prefer ceramic bowls, plates, and cutlery over paper/plastic ones?

+
All but one of the users answered ceramic bowls, plates, and cutlery.

What are three characteristics which make you prefer that type of bowls, plates, and cutlery?

+
For those who answered that they enjoyed ceramic plates they list the following factors: cutlery and crockery is more rigid and bigger and reuseable. The user who answered paper said that it was easier to throw away after their meal.

What are the factors which affect your speed?

+
Users said that speed was affected by how many people were in the Ratty, the number of dishes they had, and wether or not they were walking with friends. They explained all these factors slow down the proccess. The user who uses paper plates said that it was fast to throw away their food.

Do you move linearly through the Ratty food disposal system? Explain the factors which affect your movement.

+
Both reuseable cutlery and crockery users said that they had to place their cups down and then move back to the beggning to compost their food. The user who uses paper plates travels linearly and just throws away their cutlery and crockery.

Give one positive and one negative piece of feedback regarding the Ratty food disposal system.

+
Users enjoyed the music in the Ratty, that we are composting our food, and when there are staff members helping with food disposal. Users did not like that cups did not come first, that the spaces between the statsions were so close, and that some other users do not fully scrape their plates.
